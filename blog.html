I’m currently seeking new opportunities in front-End development and UI/UX design.

My journey to coding is a bit of a strange one... To find out more you can read my blogs.
I'm a first generation immigrant who grew up in a low income area. With this comes pressure to succeed by taking the safest route possible- become a doctor, lawyer or work for the government. Front-End development or design weren't even on my list of possible careers.
Despite all barriers I had worked incredibly hard, obtained a degree in Psychology, a Masters in Political Psychology of International relations and even interned for the United Nations!
But, I felt lost. I was still deciding on which career path I should take, the one I am expected to or the one that will make me happy?
One spring afternoon, I was incredibly bored and I decided to watch a Netflix series called "Startup". Prior to this, I had no idea hackathons existed, it seemed so exciting. I began to research, watch tutorials and joined some introductory coding classes. I was eager to learn and fell in love with front-End development and I 've been taking Shecodes workshops for the past 6 months where I became skilled in HTML, CSS, Javascript and React.
I am confident with my coding skills, I have joined a handful of hackathons, met some amazing people and worked on some cool projects (some which will help people!).
Currently, I aim to complete the Google UX course so that I can brush up on my design skills and teach myself some Python.
